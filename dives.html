Dive Site

  • Gale Reef

    Gales Reef is a reef complex in Maragogi, Brazil, that's made up of natural pools formed by the world's third-largest coral reef: Location: Located at the northernmost point of the Alagoas Gold Coast Features: Includes three natural pools: Galé Principal, Taocas, and Barra Grande Activities: Snorkeling, scuba diving, swimming, and relaxing on the beach Tours: Tours from Maceió include roundtrip transportation, a professional guide, food and drinks, and optional lunch Access: You can reach the reef by taking a catamaran ride from the beach Cost: The catamaran ride costs R$ 50, and diving costs R$ 80 What to expect: The tides change throughout the week, so each visit is unique

  • Dawson’s Reef

    Dawson Reef is a World Heritage listed area located in the Great Barrier Reef, within 10 minutes of Cooktown, the closest town to the reef. It's home to a variety of fish species, including coral trout, nannygai, tuna, mackerel, barra, jacks, GT's, queenfish, big fingermark, and tarpon. Other wildlife that can be found in the area includes turtles, sharks, and dugongs.
